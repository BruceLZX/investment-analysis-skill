# Geographic Market Context Lens

Use this file when the company, customers, suppliers, competitors, capital markets, or exit paths span different countries or regions. The goal is to avoid applying one market's logic to another market.

This is broader than competition. Geography and jurisdiction can change the entire investment thesis.

## Core Principle

Before analyzing market, competition, business model, valuation, risk, or exit, identify which market system you are talking about.

Separate:

- **Company home base**: incorporation, headquarters, R&D, manufacturing, data, licenses.
- **Current revenue market**: where customers actually pay today.
- **Target expansion market**: where the company claims it can grow.
- **Supplier and infrastructure market**: chips, cloud, components, instruments, manufacturing, talent, IP, data.
- **Comparable company market**: where benchmarks and valuation comps operate.
- **Capital market**: where investors, financing, listing, liquidity, and valuation multiples come from.
- **Exit market**: likely acquirers, IPO venue, strategic buyers, regulatory review.
- **Reader / investor perspective**: domestic investor, international investor, strategic buyer, public-market investor, or primary-market investor.

Do not collapse these into one generic "global market."

## Analysis Dimensions

Answer the dimensions that are material:

1. **Demand and budget**: Are customer pain, budget owner, procurement cycle, payment reliability, and willingness to pay similar across regions?
2. **Go-to-market and distribution**: Does the company need local sales, service, channel partners, hospital access, developer ecosystem, government qualification, or after-sales support?
3. **Pricing and unit economics**: Are ASP, gross margin, labor cost, logistics, tariffs, cloud cost, reimbursement, or subsidy economics different by geography?
4. **Product localization**: Are language, workflow, standards, certification, integration, data residency, safety, or support requirements market-specific?
5. **Competition and substitutes**: Who competes in the same buyer process today? Who is only a foreign comparable, future entrant, substitute, or valuation benchmark?
6. **Supply chain and ecosystem**: Does the company depend on region-specific suppliers, talent, manufacturing clusters, research networks, standards bodies, or platform ecosystems?
7. **Policy and regulation**: Which jurisdictions affect licenses, procurement, subsidies, export controls, sanctions, data, antitrust, reimbursement, or safety approval? Use `policy_geopolitics_lens.md` if material.
8. **Capital and valuation**: Are comps from the same market system? Adjust for liquidity, disclosure, governance, growth quality, country risk, listing venue, and exit optionality.
9. **Exit path**: Are likely acquirers domestic or international? Are foreign buyer limits, national-security review, sector approval, or listing restrictions relevant?
10. **Macro and currency**: Do FX, rates, inflation, fiscal budgets, trade cycles, or geopolitical shocks affect revenue, cost, financing, or valuation?

## Competitor / Comparable Classification

When foreign or adjacent companies appear in research, classify them:

| Type | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Direct competitor | Competes for the same buyer, budget, use case, geography, and procurement path today. |
| Local substitute | Solves the same pain through a different product, channel, or service model in the same market. |
| International competitor | Competes elsewhere and could enter if barriers are solved. |
| Strategic comparable | Useful for margins, valuation, technology route, or exit logic, but not a current competitor. |
| Future entrant | Could become a competitor in 12-36 months. |
| Non-comparable | Similar label/category, but different customer, market access, regulation, or business model. |

## Report Integration

Do not add a generic geography essay. Integrate the lens into:

- Executive briefing: state which geography/perspective drives the conclusion.
- Market: separate global TAM, serviceable geography, and realistically obtainable market.
- Product/GTM: explain localization, channel, support, certification, and customer-access constraints.
- Business model/financials: adjust pricing, margin, sales cycle, payment risk, labor/capex, and supply-chain assumptions by region.
- Competition: separate direct competitors from foreign comparables, substitutes, future entrants, and non-comparables.
- Valuation: avoid using foreign comps without adjustment; explain why the comp is relevant or only directional.
- Risk: include market-entry, localization, supply-chain, FX, macro, regulation, policy, and exit-market risks when material.
- Exit: test whether likely acquirers/listing venues exist in the same market system.

If the lens is not material, state briefly: `地理/市场体系因素：未发现对投资结论有重大影响的跨区域差异。`
